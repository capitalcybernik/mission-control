CMMC Compliance for Construction Companies

The Army Corps of Engineers is already requiring CMMC certification on active solicitations. If you build for the federal government, this is your new reality.

Army Corps of Engineers Is Leading the Charge

USACE is the single largest source of CMMC requirements in the construction sector. Of the 15 construction solicitations we track that require CMMC, 13 come from the Army Corps of Engineers. These cover everything from design build contracts to microgrid installations to seismic upgrades.

USACE NWD Design Build MATOC

Level 2 C3PAO required. Multi award task order contract for design build work across the Northwestern Division. Posted February 2026.

Special Tactics Complex, OR Air National Guard

Level 2 C3PAO required. Military construction for special operations. This is not an IT project. It is a building, and it requires cybersecurity certification.

Ft. Buchanan Microgrid

Level 2 required. Energy infrastructure at a military installation. Even electrical and mechanical contractors are now in scope for CMMC.

The pattern is clear. USACE is embedding CMMC into contracts that, two years ago, had zero cybersecurity language. If you are a general contractor, specialty contractor, or design build firm that works on military installations, CMMC certification is no longer optional.

What CMMC Means for Your Construction Business

The practical impact is straightforward: without CMMC certification, you cannot bid on these contracts. Your proposal will be rejected before it is evaluated on merit. This applies to prime contractors and subcontractors alike.

If you are a subcontractor on a CMMC contract, the prime is required to flow down the certification requirement to you. Even if you never interact with the contracting officer directly, you will need to demonstrate compliance. Primes are already asking their subs about CMMC status because their own certification depends on it.

This also affects your competitive position. As more contracts require certification, the pool of eligible bidders shrinks. Companies that get certified early will face less competition on these contracts. Companies that wait will find themselves locked out of work they used to win routinely.

The Big 5 Controls You Need

CMMC Level 2 maps to 110 controls from NIST SP 800-171. That sounds overwhelming, but the foundation comes down to five areas where most construction companies have the biggest gaps:

Multi Factor Authentication

Every account that accesses company systems needs a second factor beyond a password. This includes email, file sharing, project management tools, and remote access.

Endpoint Detection & Response

Traditional antivirus is not enough. EDR monitors every device for suspicious activity and can isolate threats before they spread across your network.

Encrypted Backups

Your data needs to be backed up, encrypted, stored offsite, and tested regularly. If ransomware hits, you need to be able to restore operations without paying.

Security Awareness Training

Every employee needs documented, recurring training on phishing, password security, and data handling. Annual training is the minimum. Quarterly is better.

Automated Patching

Software vulnerabilities are discovered constantly. Automated patching ensures your systems are updated without waiting for someone to remember to do it manually.

These five controls won't get you to full Level 2 compliance on their own, but they close the most critical gaps and build the foundation that everything else rests on.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do subcontractors need CMMC certification?

Yes. If you handle controlled unclassified information (CUI) as a subcontractor on a DoD contract, you need CMMC certification at the level specified in the contract. Primes are required to flow this down.

What level do most construction contracts require?

The majority of current solicitations require Level 2. Some start at Level 1 with escalation clauses that move to Level 2 and Level 3 over time. A small number of high sensitivity projects require Level 2 with a C3PAO (third party) assessment from the start.

How long does it take to get CMMC certified?

We don't handle classified information. Do we still need CMMC?

CMMC is not about classified information. It covers controlled unclassified information (CUI), which includes things like building plans for military facilities, project schedules, contract details, and technical specifications. If you work on a DoD construction project, you almost certainly handle CUI.
