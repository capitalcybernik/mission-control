What This Means for Your Business

These are not draft policies or proposed rules. These are live solicitations on SAM.gov right now, each one requiring some level of CMMC certification before you can bid. If you work in construction or manufacturing and you touch DoD contracts, the window to get compliant is closing.

The ripple effect matters just as much as the direct requirements. Every prime contractor on these solicitations will need to flow down CMMC requirements to their subcontractors. If you're a subcontractor on a project like the USACE NWD Design Build MATOC, your prime will ask for proof of certification. No certification, no seat at the table.

Construction companies that have never thought about cybersecurity are suddenly discovering that the Army Corps of Engineers requires CMMC Level 2 for a microgrid installation. Manufacturers building parts for Navy submarines need C3PAO certification before they can even submit a proposal. This is the new reality of doing business with the federal government.

The Escalation Is Real

Look at the SOF Tactical Equipment Maintenance Facility solicitation. It requires Level 1 today, Level 2 by October 2026, and Level 3 by October 2027. This is the pattern DoD is setting: start with a baseline and ratchet up. Waiting until the higher levels are required means you'll be scrambling while your competitors are already certified.
