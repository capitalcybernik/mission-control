Frequently Asked Questions

We're a subcontractor. Do we still need CMMC?

Yes. If you handle CUI as part of a DoD supply chain, you need CMMC certification at the level required by the prime contract. Primes are required to flow down CMMC requirements to all subcontractors handling CUI.

What level do manufacturing contracts typically require?

Most current solicitations require Level 2. Some allow self assessment, while others (particularly Navy NAVSEA contracts) require C3PAO third party certification. Level 1 is less common and generally applies to contracts with limited CUI exposure.

What about our CNC machines and shop floor systems?

Legacy industrial systems are a known challenge in CMMC compliance. The key is network segmentation (isolating shop floor systems from your business network), documenting compensating controls for systems that cannot be patched, and ensuring CUI is protected at every point in your workflow. Capital Cyber has specific experience with manufacturing environments.

How long does compliance take for a manufacturer?

The Big 5 controls go in within 30 days through our Fast Track program. Full assessment readiness typically takes 60 to 90 days depending on the complexity of your environment, particularly if you have extensive shop floor systems that need segmentation or compensating controls.
