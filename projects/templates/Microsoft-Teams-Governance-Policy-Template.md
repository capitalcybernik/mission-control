## 1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes guidelines for the use, management, and governance of Microsoft Teams across the organization. The goal is to maintain an organized, secure, and efficient collaboration environment while giving employees the flexibility to communicate and collaborate effectively.

---

## 2. SCOPE

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and authorized users who access Microsoft Teams through the organization's Microsoft 365 tenant.

---

## 3. TEAM AND CHANNEL CREATION

### 3.1 Creation Permissions

All users are permitted to create new Teams and channels. There are no restrictions on team or channel creation. This approach encourages collaboration and allows departments to self organize based on their operational needs.

### 3.2 Naming Conventions

To maintain an organized environment, all new Teams should follow a consistent naming structure:

**Recommended Format:** [Department or Location] [Purpose]

**Examples:**
- HR Benefits Discussion
- IT Support Requests
- Sales Weekly Pipeline
- Marketing Campaign Planning
- Operations Daily Standup

**Guidelines:**
- Use clear, descriptive names that identify the team's purpose at a glance
- Avoid vague or generic names such as "Test," "New Team," or "Misc"
- Include the department or location when the team is specific to a group
- Do not use special characters or emojis in team names

### 3.3 Team Descriptions

When creating a new team, the creator should include a brief description of the team's purpose. This helps other users understand the team's function before requesting to join.

---

## 4. TEAM OWNERSHIP AND MANAGEMENT

### 4.1 Team Owners

Every team must have at least **two (2) active owners**. Team owners are responsible for:

- Managing team membership (adding and removing members)
- Monitoring channel activity and content
- Archiving or deleting the team when it is no longer needed
- Ensuring the team adheres to this governance policy

### 4.2 Ownerless Teams

If a team owner leaves the organization or becomes inactive, IT administration will assign a replacement owner. Managers are responsible for notifying IT when a team owner departs so ownership can be transferred promptly.

### 4.3 Periodic Review

Team owners should review their team membership on a quarterly basis to ensure only current, relevant members have access. Former employees, transferred staff, or users who no longer need access should be removed.

---

## 5. DATA BACKUP AND RETENTION

### 5.1 Microsoft Teams Channels

**Teams channels and their associated chat data are not included in the organization's backup solution** unless a specific backup request is submitted to IT.

Teams chat and channel data is retained per Microsoft 365's default retention settings, but is not externally backed up. If a department or team requires backup of their Teams data, they must submit a request to IT specifying:

- The team name
- The business justification for the backup request
- The desired retention period

### 5.2 SharePoint Sites

Departments that utilize dedicated SharePoint sites for document storage and collaboration may have those sites backed up as part of the organization's data protection strategy. Backup of SharePoint sites is configured on a per department basis and must be requested through IT.

**What is included in SharePoint backups (when configured):**
- All documents and files stored on the SharePoint site
- Document libraries and folder structures
- Version history

Backup schedules and retention periods are managed by the IT service provider per the organization's backup policy.

### 5.3 Teams Call Recordings

All Microsoft Teams call recordings are retained for **365 days** from the date of the recording. After 365 days, call recordings are **automatically deleted**.

**Important notes regarding call recordings:**
- Recordings of one on one calls are stored in the recording initiator's OneDrive
- Recordings of channel meetings are stored in the associated SharePoint site
- Users are responsible for downloading any recordings they need to retain beyond the 365 day retention period before expiration
- Call recordings may contain sensitive or confidential information and should be treated accordingly
- Do not share call recordings with external parties without management approval

### 5.4 Team Expiration Policy

There is **no automatic expiration policy** applied to Teams. Teams will not be automatically deleted or archived due to inactivity.

Team owners are responsible for managing the lifecycle of their teams, including archiving or deleting teams that are no longer in use (see Section 6).

## 6. TEAM LIFECYCLE MANAGEMENT

### 6.1 Creating a Team

Before creating a new team, users should:

1. Search existing teams to confirm a similar team does not already exist
2. Choose a clear, descriptive name following the naming conventions in Section 3.2
3. Add a description of the team's purpose
4. Assign at least two owners
5. Add relevant members

### 6.2 Maintaining a Team

Active team owners should:

- Remove members who no longer need access
- Keep the team name and description current
- Organize content into appropriate channels
- Pin important resources or documents for easy access

### 6.3 Archiving a Team

When a team is no longer active but contains useful reference material, the owner should **archive** the team. Archiving preserves all content in a read only state while removing it from active view.

**To archive a team:**
1. Click the three dots (**...**) next to the team name
2. Select **Manage team**
3. Click **Settings**
4. Select **Archive this team**
5. Optionally check "Make the SharePoint site read only" to lock associated files

Archived teams can be restored at any time by an owner or IT administrator.

### 6.4 Deleting a Team

Teams that are no longer needed and contain no important data should be deleted by the team owner. Before deleting, the owner should:

1. Confirm with team members that no critical data needs to be preserved
2. Download or relocate any important files
3. Proceed with deletion

**Deleted teams can be restored within 30 days** by an IT administrator. After 30 days, the team and all associated data are permanently removed.

## 7. SECURITY AND COMPLIANCE

### 7.1 External Guest Access

- Do not add external guests to Teams without management approval
- Sensitive company data should not be shared in Teams channels accessible to external users
- If external collaboration is required, contact IT to configure guest access appropriately
- Guest access should be reviewed quarterly and removed when no longer needed

### 7.2 Sensitive Information

The following types of information should **never** be shared in Teams chats or channels:

- Passwords or access credentials
- Social Security numbers
- Credit card or bank account information
- Protected health information (PHI)
- Any data classified as confidential under company policy

Use approved secure file sharing solutions for sensitive documents.

### 7.3 Suspicious Activity

Report any suspicious messages, phishing attempts, or unusual activity in Teams to IT immediately. Do not click on links or open attachments from unknown or suspicious sources within Teams.

### 7.4 Personal Use

Microsoft Teams is a business tool provided for work related communication and collaboration. Limited personal use is acceptable, but the platform should not be used for activities that violate company policy or create legal liability for the organization.

---

## 8. USER RESPONSIBILITIES

All Microsoft Teams users are expected to:

- Follow the naming conventions outlined in this policy
- Keep their teams and channels organized
- **Hide** teams they no longer actively use for a cleaner workspace
- Report inactive or ownerless teams to IT
- Comply with all security and data handling guidelines
- Download important call recordings before the 365 day retention period expires

---

## 9. IT ADMINISTRATION

IT administration reserves the right to:

- Reassign team ownership when owners leave the organization
- Remove teams that violate this policy after appropriate notice
- Audit team membership and activity for security and compliance purposes
- Modify this policy as organizational needs evolve
