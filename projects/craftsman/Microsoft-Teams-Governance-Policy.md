## 1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes guidelines for the use, management, and governance of Microsoft Teams within Craftsman Auto Care. The goal is to maintain an organized, secure, and efficient collaboration environment while giving employees the flexibility to create and manage their own workspaces.

---

## 2. SCOPE

This policy applies to all Craftsman Auto Care employees, contractors, and authorized users who access Microsoft Teams through the organization's Microsoft 365 tenant.

---

## 3. TEAM AND CHANNEL CREATION

### 3.1 Who Can Create Teams and Channels

All employees are permitted to create new Teams and channels. There are no restrictions on team or channel creation. This policy encourages collaboration and allows teams to self organize based on their operational needs.

### 3.2 Naming Conventions

To keep the environment organized and easy to navigate, all new Teams should follow these naming guidelines:

**Format:** [Location or Department] [Purpose]

**Examples:**
- Sterling Service Desk
- HR Benefits Discussion
- All Stores Weekly Updates
- Chantilly Parts Ordering
- IT Support Requests

**Guidelines:**
- Use clear, descriptive names that identify the team's purpose at a glance
- Avoid vague names like "Test," "New Team," or "Misc"
- Include the store location name when the team is specific to one location
- Do not include special characters or emojis in team names

### 3.3 Team Descriptions

When creating a new team, the creator should include a brief description of the team's purpose. This helps other employees understand what the team is for before joining.

---

## 4. TEAM OWNERSHIP AND MANAGEMENT

### 4.1 Team Owners

Every team should have at least **two (2) active owners**. Team owners are responsible for:

- Managing team membership (adding and removing members)
- Monitoring channel activity and content
- Archiving or deleting the team when it is no longer needed
- Ensuring the team follows this governance policy

### 4.2 Ownerless Teams

If a team owner leaves the organization or becomes inactive, IT will assign a new owner. Managers are responsible for notifying IT when a team owner departs so ownership can be transferred promptly.

---

## 5. DATA BACKUP AND RETENTION

### 5.1 Microsoft Teams Channels

**Teams channels and their associated chat data are NOT backed up** unless a specific backup request is submitted to IT. Teams chat and channel data is retained per Microsoft 365's default retention settings, but it is not included in the organization's external backup solution.

If a department or team requires backup of their Teams data, they must submit a request to **ticket@craftsmanautocare.com** specifying:

- The team name
- The reason for the backup request
- The desired retention period

### 5.2 SharePoint Sites (Contracts Department)

The **Contracts department** uses dedicated SharePoint sites for document storage and collaboration. These SharePoint sites **are backed up to Rubrik** as part of the organization's data protection strategy.

**What is backed up:**
- All documents and files stored on Contracts department SharePoint sites
- Document libraries and folder structures
- Version history

**Backup schedule and retention** are managed by Capital Cyber per the organization's backup policy.

### 5.3 Teams Call Recordings

All Microsoft Teams call recordings are stored for **365 days** from the date of the recording. After 365 days, call recordings are automatically deleted.

**Important notes about call recordings:**
- Recordings are stored in OneDrive (for 1:1 calls) or SharePoint (for channel meetings)
- Users should download any recordings they need to keep beyond 365 days before the retention period expires
- Call recordings may contain sensitive information and should be treated accordingly
- Do not share call recordings with external parties without management approval

### 5.4 No Expiration Policy on Teams

There is **no automatic expiration policy** on Teams. Teams will not be automatically deleted or archived due to inactivity. Team owners are responsible for archiving or removing teams that are no longer in use.

---

## 6. TEAM LIFECYCLE MANAGEMENT

### 6.1 Active Teams

Teams that are actively used require no action. Owners should periodically review membership to ensure only current, relevant members have access.

### 6.2 Inactive Teams

While there is no automatic expiration, team owners are encouraged to:

- **Archive** teams that are no longer active but may contain useful reference material. Archiving preserves all content in a read only state.
- **Delete** teams that are no longer needed and contain no important data.

### 6.3 How to Archive a Team

1. Click the three dots (**...**) next to the team name
2. Select **Manage team**
3. Click **Settings**
4. Select **Archive this team**
5. Optionally check "Make the SharePoint site read only" to lock associated files

Archived teams can be restored at any time by an owner or IT administrator.

---

## 7. SECURITY AND COMPLIANCE

### 7.1 External Sharing

- Do not add external guests to Teams without management approval
- Sensitive company data should not be shared in Teams channels accessible to external users
- If external collaboration is required, contact IT to set up a guest access request

### 7.2 Sensitive Information

- Do not share passwords, Social Security numbers, credit card information, or other sensitive personal data in Teams chats or channels
- Use SharePoint or approved secure file sharing for sensitive documents
- Report any suspicious messages or phishing attempts in Teams to **ticket@craftsmanautocare.com**

### 7.3 Personal Use

Microsoft Teams is a business tool provided for work related communication and collaboration. Limited personal use is acceptable, but the platform should not be used for activities that violate company policy or create legal liability.
