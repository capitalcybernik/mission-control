# Viral LinkedIn Post Pattern

## Source: Nikhil's reference post (Anthropic/surveillance angle)

## Structure
1. **Hook line** — bold, confrontational, stops the scroll
2. **Authority source** — name drop who said it (credibility)
3. **Reframe** — "Read that again" / "Let that sink in" moment
4. **Evidence stack** — checkmark emoji bullet list of specific facts
5. **Escalation** — "And here's the scary part..."
6. **Paint the picture** — describe the real world consequence
7. **Pivot to action** — "This is why [thing] matters"
8. **Repeat pivot** — stack multiple "This is why..." lines
9. **Close with weight** — leave them thinking

## Rules
- ONE thought per line. Max two sentences.
- Line break after EVERY thought. White space is your weapon.
- Plain language. No jargon. No corporate speak.
- Talk TO the reader. "You" not "one" or "people."
- Use rhetorical questions that answer themselves.
- ✅ Checkmark emojis for evidence lists
- Build tension like a staircase. Each line raises the stakes.
- Use repetition for rhythm: "This is why... This is why... This is why..."
- Confrontational but not preachy. Inform, don't lecture.
- End before you think you should. Leave them wanting more.

## Tone
- Urgent but not panicked
- Insider knowledge vibe ("they admitted this")
- "I've been telling you" energy
- Conversational, like texting a friend who needs to wake up

## What NOT to do
- No long paragraphs
- No hedge words ("perhaps," "might," "could potentially")
- No filler ("In today's rapidly evolving landscape...")
- No passive voice
- No corporate LinkedIn fluff
- No hashtag spam at the end

## Example Cadence
```
Bold claim.

Source/authority.

One line context.

"Read that again."

Here's what they said:

✅ Fact one
✅ Fact two  
✅ Fact three

Without [protection/oversight/action].

And here's the scary part...

[Consequence described vividly]

Every [thing].
Every [thing].
Every [thing].

All of it.

[Short punchy summary].

The law hasn't caught up yet.

This is why [specific thing] matters.
This is why [specific thing] matters.
This is why [specific thing] matters.

Because [closing weight].
```
